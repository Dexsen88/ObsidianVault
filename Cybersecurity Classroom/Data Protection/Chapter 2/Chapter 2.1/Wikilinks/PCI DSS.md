The **Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)** is a mandatory global information security standard administered by the Payment Card Industry Security Standards Council (PCI SSC), which includes major card brands (Visa, Mastercard, etc.).

- **Goal:** To protect cardholder data (CHD) wherever it is stored, processed, or transmitted, reducing card fraud.
    
- **Requirement:** Applies to all merchants, processors, and service providers that handle CHD. Compliance is enforced through contractual agreements with card brands.
    
- **Controls:** Includes 12 core requirements, such as building and maintaining a secure network, protecting data with strong **[[Encryption]]**, using **[[Malware]]** protection, and regularly testing security systems. **[[Tokenization]]** is a key strategy for reducing the scope of compliance.
