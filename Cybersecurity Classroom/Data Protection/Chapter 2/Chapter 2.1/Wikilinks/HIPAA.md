The **Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)** is a United States federal law that governs the security and privacy of healthcare information.

- **Goal:** To establish national standards for the protection of certain health information, known as **Protected Health Information (PHI)**.
    
- **Key Components:**
    
    - **Privacy Rule:** Defines how PHI can be used and disclosed.
        
    - **Security Rule:** Mandates administrative, physical, and technical safeguards (like **[[Encryption]]** and **[[Access Control]]**) to protect electronic PHI (ePHI).
        
- **Compliance:** Failure to comply with HIPAA can result in significant financial penalties, making it a critical **[[Data Protection]]** requirement for the healthcare sector.
